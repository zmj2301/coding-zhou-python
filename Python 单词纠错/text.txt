Last semester, I joined the school’s photography club because I have always been interesting in capturing beautiful moments. At first, I was nervous to talk with other members, as I thought my skills were to poor. However, everyone was so friendly that they encouraged me to share my photoes.​
Our club held many activitys every month. Once, we went to a park for an outdoor shooting. I forgot to bring my camera battery, which made me feel embarrased. Luckily, a senior lent me his spare one. He also taught me how to adjust the settigns to take better pictures. I learned that patience is key—good photos often require waiting for the right light and angle.​
Another memorable event was our school’s photo exhibition. I spent three weekends editing my works, but I almost missed the deadline because I miscalculated the time. With the help of my teammates, we finished preparing the display boards just in time. Seeing my photos hung on the wall, I felt a strong sense of achievemnt.​
Joining the photography club not only improved my skills but also helped me make many good friendes. I realized that making mistakes is part of learning, and asking for help is not a sign of weakness. This experience taught me to be more confident and open-minded. I will cherish these memories forever and keep pursuing my hobby of photography.
